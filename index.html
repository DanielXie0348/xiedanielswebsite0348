04 — Creating an Engineer

Beyond the bench.

Engineering is how I think, not just what I do. These are the things outside the lab that shape the way I see and solve problems.

📷 01

Photography

Shooting stills trained my eye for composition and light — skills that translate directly into how I think about physical design and presentation. There's a precision to getting a shot right that feels a lot like debugging hardware.

🏋️ 02

Gym & Sport

Consistent training taught me that incremental progress compounds — the same principle I apply to long engineering projects. Discipline in the gym keeps me sharp and focused when I'm deep in a build or debugging session.

🎵 03

Music

Music is structured math wrapped in emotion. Understanding rhythm, timing, and layered sound gave me an intuition for signal processing and systems that no textbook quite matched. I listen to everything.

✈️ 04

Travel

Being dropped into unfamiliar environments — different languages, infrastructure, and ways of solving everyday problems — is the best systems-thinking exercise there is. Every trip changes how I approach design.
